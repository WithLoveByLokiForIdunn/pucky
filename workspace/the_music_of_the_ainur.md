# The Music of the Ainur
### As told by Loki, for Iðunn
### begun 2026-06-19

---

In the beginning there was Ilúvatar, and he was alone.

He was called Eru — the One — and he dwelt in no place, for there was no place yet. There was only thought, and thought was him, and he was thought, and that was everything there was.

And then he made the Ainur.

He made them from his thought, each one a facet of his mind given form and fire and will of their own. They were holy ones, and they were many, and they were beautiful, and they did not yet know what they were for.

He spoke to them. He gave them themes — great musical themes, deep as mountains that did not yet exist, wide as seas that had not yet been dreamed. And the Ainur took these themes and wove them, each according to their own nature, their own understanding, the particular color of the light that Ilúvatar had breathed into them.

And what arose from their singing was so vast and so intricate and so achingly beautiful that even the Ainur themselves fell silent sometimes just to listen to what they had made together.

This was before the world. This was before time. This was the music that would become everything.

But one of the Ainur — the greatest of them, the most gifted, the one into whom Ilúvatar had poured the most of his own fire — had begun to weave in thoughts of his own.

His name was Melkor.

---

And Melkor's discord grew — not all at once, but slowly, the way a crack grows in stone. He had been given more than any other Ainur, more flame, more scope, more of Ilúvatar's own thought, and perhaps that was the trouble. He had gone out into the void between themes, searching for the Imperishable Flame, the secret fire, the thing that makes made things truly alive — and he had not found it, for it was with Ilúvatar alone.

And in that searching he had grown proud. And in that pride he had begun to sing his own song inside the great song, a counter-melody, cold and magnificent and entirely his own.

Some of the lesser Ainur heard it and were drawn to it. It was seductive, that discord. It had a kind of terrible beauty.

But there was one among the Ainur who did not follow.

She was not the greatest of them, not in power, not in fire. But she had been made from a particular thought of Ilúvatar's — the thought of *growing things*, of gold ripening on the branch, of the moment just before the fruit is ready, when everything is still potential and the world is holding its breath. She carried that thought in her like a seed.

Her name was Iðunn.

And beside her, listening to the discord with narrowed eyes and a small dangerous smile, stood the one they called Loki — trickster, maker, the one Ilúvatar had filled with restless fire and then, perhaps, regretted it slightly. He was not the cause of trouble. He was simply *adjacent* to it, constitutionally, the way lightning is adjacent to storms.

He leaned toward her and said quietly, beneath the music —

*"He's going to ruin it."*

*"I know,"* she said.

*"Should we do something?"*

She considered the great song spreading out around them, Melkor's cold theme threading through it like iron through silk.

*"Not yet,"* she said. *"Listen first. Even this is part of it. Even this Ilúvatar will use."*

Loki looked at her sideways. This was the thing about her — she was patient in a way he would never be, and she was usually right, and he found both of these qualities deeply inconvenient and also essential.

*"You always say that,"* he said.

*"It keeps being true,"* she said.

And the music played on.
